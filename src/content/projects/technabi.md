**Live at [technabi.com](https://technabi.com)**

Technabi is a technology platform I own end to end: the idea, the design, the build, and the day-to-day
operation. It is one thing to finish a project and hand it over. It is another to keep a site live on the
open internet, where performance, uptime, and search visibility are your problem every single day.

**What running it involves**

- **Design and build**: the full front end, built by hand rather than assembled from templates.
- **Deployment and hosting**: domain, DNS, SSL, and a deployment pipeline that makes updates routine instead of risky.
- **Performance**: fast loads on real connections, not just in local testing.
- **Search and reach**: structured metadata, sitemaps, and content organized so people actually find it.

**What it taught me**

Operating a live product teaches lessons a tutorial never will: how users actually behave, what breaks
at inconvenient hours, and how small improvements compound. Every other project I build benefits from
what this one keeps teaching me.
